Our Story & Commitment

25 years of connecting the world with authentic East Africaan flavors

From Nairobi Markets to Global Kitchens

Founded in 1995 by John Kamau, Aripak East Africa began as a small stall in Nairobi's bustling spice market. What started as a family business sourcing spices for local restaurants has grown into an internationally recognized brand, while maintaining our commitment to quality and community.

Today, we work directly with over 200 small-scale farmers across Kenya, providing them with fair prices and sustainable farming training. Our products now grace kitchens in 15 countries worldwide, yet we remain rooted in our Nairobi origins.

Our Corporate Social Responsibility

We measure success not just in profits, but in positive impact

Promoting Sustainable Agriculture

We invest in training programs that teach farmers regenerative agriculture techniques. Our initiatives have helped convert over 500 acres to organic farming methods, protecting East Africa's delicate ecosystems while improving yields.

  • 500+ acres converted to organic
  • 30% average yield increase for farmers
  • 100% chemical-free farming by 2025 goal

Supporting Local Education

5% of all profits fund educational initiatives in farming communities. We've built 3 schools, provided scholarships for 120 students, and distribute school supplies annually to children in our partner communities.

  • 3 schools built since 2010
  • 120 university scholarships awarded
  • 5,000+ school kits distributed annually

Empowering Women Farmers

60% of our partner farmers are women. We provide special training programs, microloans, and leadership development to help women build sustainable businesses and gain financial independence.

  • 60% of farmers are women
  • 200+ microloans provided
  • 15 women-led cooperatives established
